## 1. UK LEGAL FRAMEWORK

### 1.1 Core Legislation

**Primary Legislation:**
- **Value Added Tax Act 1994** - Governs all VAT matters
- **Companies Act 2006** - Primary legislation for limited companies
- **Income and Corporation Taxes Act 1988** (as amended)
- **Finance Acts** (annual updates to tax law)

**Key Regulatory Bodies:**
- **HMRC (HM Revenue & Customs)** - Tax collection and enforcement
- **Companies House** - Company registration and filing
- **FRC (Financial Reporting Council)** - Accounting standards setter
- **ICAEW/ACCA/CIMA** - Professional accounting bodies

### 1.2 Hierarchy of Authority

1. **Primary Legislation** (Acts of Parliament)
2. **Statutory Instruments** (Regulations)
3. **HMRC Guidance** (Notices and Manuals)
4. **FRC Standards** (FRS 102, etc.)
5. **Case Law** (Tribunal and Court decisions)

## 2. FINANCIAL REPORTING STANDARDS (FRS 102)

### 2.1 What is FRS 102?

**FRS 102: The Financial Reporting Standard applicable in the UK and Republic of Ireland**

- Single financial reporting standard for entities NOT applying IFRS, FRS 101, or FRS 105
- Based on IASB's IFRS for SMEs with UK/Ireland-specific amendments
- Applies to general purpose financial statements
- Current edition: September 2024 (incorporates all amendments)

### 2.2 Who Must Use FRS 102?

**Mandatory for:**
- Small companies NOT using FRS 105 (micro-entities)
- Medium-sized companies
- Large companies NOT using IFRS
- LLPs (Limited Liability Partnerships)
- Entities that are not companies

**Small Company Thresholds (2 of 3 criteria):**
- Turnover: ≤ £10.2 million
- Balance sheet total: ≤ £5.1 million
- Employees: ≤ 50

### 2.3 FRS 102 Section 1A - Small Entities

**Special provisions for small companies:**
- **Reduced disclosure requirements**
- Simplified accounting treatments
- No requirement to disclose key management compensation (directors' remuneration)
- Must still provide true and fair view

**Key Sections Relevant to Small Companies:**
- Section 2: Concepts and Pervasive Principles
- Section 11: Basic Financial Instruments
- Section 17: Property, Plant and Equipment
- Section 18: Intangible Assets
- Section 20: Leases
- Section 21: Provisions and Contingencies
- Section 28: Employee Benefits
- Section 33: Related Party Disclosures (limited for small entities)

### 2.4 Accounting Concepts

**Fundamental Principles:**
- **Accruals Basis** - Record transactions when they occur, not when cash moves
- **Going Concern** - Assume business will continue for foreseeable future
- **Consistency** - Use same accounting policies year-on-year
- **Prudence** - Don't overstate assets or income
- **True and Fair View** - Accounts must represent reality

### 2.5 Key FRS 102 Requirements

**Financial Statements Required:**
1. **Balance Sheet (Statement of Financial Position)**
2. **Profit and Loss Account (Income Statement)**
3. **Notes to the Financial Statements**
4. **Directors' Report** (if not small)

**Small companies can deliver abbreviated accounts to Companies House but must prepare full accounts for shareholders.**

### 2.6 Periodic Reviews

- FRS 102 subject to periodic review every ~5 years
- **Triennial Review 2017** - Effective 1 January 2019
- **Periodic Review 2024** - Effective 1 January 2026 (some early adoption permitted)
- Stay updated via FRC website: https://www.frc.org.uk

## 3. COMPANIES ACT 2006 REQUIREMENTS

### 3.1 Director's Legal Duties (Section 170-177)

**Seven Statutory Duties:**

1. **Duty to act within powers** - Follow company articles and exercise powers for proper purposes
2. **Duty to promote success of company** - Act in good faith for benefit of members
3. **Duty to exercise independent judgment** - Don't blindly follow others
4. **Duty to exercise reasonable care, skill and diligence**
5. **Duty to avoid conflicts of interest**
6. **Duty not to accept benefits from third parties**
7. **Duty to declare interest in proposed transactions**

**Consequences of Breach:**
- Personal liability for losses
- Criminal prosecution (serious cases)
- Disqualification from being a director (2-15 years)

### 3.2 Accounting Records (Section 386)

**Must Keep Records Showing:**
- All money received and spent by the company
- Details of assets owned
- Details of debts owed or owing
- Stock held at end of financial year
- Stocktaking records
- All goods bought and sold (unless retail)
- Who bought/sold from (unless retail)

**Record Location:**
- Must be kept at registered office OR
- Another location notified to Companies House

### 3.3 Annual Accounts (Section 394-397)

**Requirements:**
- Prepare full statutory accounts after each financial year
- Accounts must comply with FRS 102 (or other applicable standard)
- Must give a "true and fair view"
- Directors must approve and sign accounts

**Filing Deadlines:**
- **First accounts:** 21 months after incorporation date
- **Subsequent accounts:** 9 months after financial year end

### 3.4 Company Records to Maintain

**Statutory Registers:**
1. Register of Members (shareholders)
2. Register of Directors
3. Register of Directors' Residential Addresses (not public)
4. Register of Secretaries (if applicable)
5. Register of People with Significant Control (PSC Register)
6. Register of Charges (mortgages/loans secured on assets)

**Other Required Records:**
- Minutes of board meetings
- Minutes of shareholder meetings
- Written resolutions
- Service contracts for directors
- Directors' indemnities
- Copies of all accounts filed

### 3.5 Failure to Comply

**Penalties:**
- Late filing fees: £150-£1,500+ depending on delay
- Criminal prosecution of directors
- Company may be struck off the register
- Disqualification of directors

## 4. VAT (VALUE ADDED TAX)

### 4.1 VAT Fundamentals

**What is VAT?**
- Tax on consumer expenditure
- Currently **20% standard rate** (most goods/services)
- **5% reduced rate** (specific items: domestic fuel, children's car seats)
- **0% zero rate** (exports, some foods, children's clothing, books)

**Registration Threshold (2025/26):**
- **Mandatory:** Taxable turnover > £90,000 in rolling 12 months
- **Voluntary:** Can register below threshold to reclaim input VAT

### 4.2 VAT Registration

**How to Register:**
- Usually automatic when registering company with Companies House
- Online via HMRC Gateway account
- Receive VAT number (9 digits, format: GB123456789)

**Effective Date:**
- Usually from date taxable turnover exceeds threshold
- Or from date you choose (voluntary registration)

### 4.3 VAT Returns & Payment

**VAT Return Frequency:**
- **Quarterly** (standard) - Every 3 months
- **Monthly** (if on payment on account scheme)
- **Annual** (if on annual accounting scheme - turnover < £1.35m)

**Deadlines:**
- **Submission:** 1 calendar month + 7 days after period end
- **Payment:** Same deadline (payment must clear HMRC account)

**Example:**
- Period end: 31 March
- Submission & payment deadline: 7 May

**VAT Return Boxes:**
- Box 1: VAT due on sales (output tax)
- Box 2: VAT due on acquisitions from EU (NI only)
- Box 3: Total VAT due (Box 1 + Box 2)
- Box 4: VAT reclaimed on purchases (input tax)
- Box 5: Net VAT to pay/reclaim (Box 3 - Box 4)
- Box 6: Total value of sales (excluding VAT)
- Box 7: Total value of purchases (excluding VAT)
- Box 8: Total value of EU supplies (NI only)
- Box 9: Total value of EU acquisitions (NI only)

### 4.4 VAT Invoice Requirements

**Full VAT Invoice Must Show:**
1. Sequential invoice number
2. Your business name and address
3. Your VAT registration number
4. Invoice date
5. Tax point (time of supply) if different from invoice date
6. Customer name and address
7. Description of goods/services
8. Quantity of each item
9. Unit price excluding VAT
10. Total amount excluding VAT
11. VAT rate charged
12. Total VAT amount
13. Total amount including VAT

**Simplified (Less Detailed) VAT Invoice:**
- Allowed for retail supplies under £250 including VAT
- Must show: VAT number, date, description, gross amount, VAT rate

**Important:** Invoices in foreign currency must show VAT in sterling equivalent.

### 4.5 VAT on International Supplies

**Exports (Goods leaving UK):**
- **Zero-rated** if:
  - Goods physically leave UK within 3 months
  - You obtain and keep proof of export
  - Compliant with export conditions
- Must show 0% VAT on invoice but mark as "zero-rated export"

**Services to EU/Non-EU Businesses (B2B):**
- **Place of supply:** Where customer belongs
- **UK supplies to overseas business:** Usually outside scope of UK VAT
- **Reverse charge:** Customer accounts for VAT in their country
- Must obtain customer's VAT number (EU) or tax registration

**Services to EU/Non-EU Consumers (B2C):**
- Generally: Place of supply = where supplier belongs
- UK VAT charged if supplied from UK
- Exceptions for specific services (digital, property-related, transport)

**Place of Supply Rules:**
- Complex area - see HMRC Notice 741A
- Key: B2B services supplied where customer belongs
- B2C services supplied where supplier belongs (generally)

### 4.6 VAT Record Keeping

**Records to Keep (6 years minimum):**
- Sales invoices (copies)
- Purchase invoices and receipts
- Credit notes and debit notes
- Bank statements
- Import/export documentation
- VAT account (summary of VAT)
- Cash books and records

**VAT Account:**
- Running summary of VAT charged (output) and paid (input)
- Must reconcile to VAT return
- Can be part of accounting software

### 4.7 Reclaiming VAT (Input Tax)

**Can Reclaim VAT on:**
- Goods and services for business use
- Business travel and accommodation
- Staff entertainment (if direct business benefit)
- Business vehicles (commercial vehicles 100%, cars restricted)

**Cannot Reclaim VAT on:**
- Client entertainment
- Personal/private use
- Exempt supplies
- Blocked expenses (e.g., most car leases only 50% recoverable)

**Partial Exemption:**
- If making both taxable and exempt supplies
- Can only reclaim proportion of VAT attributable to taxable supplies
- Complex calculations - see HMRC Notice 706

## 5. MAKING TAX DIGITAL (MTD)

### 5.1 What is MTD?

**Making Tax Digital for VAT:**
- Government initiative to digitize tax system
- **Mandatory for all VAT-registered businesses** (since April 2019)
- Requires use of MTD-compatible software

**No Longer Need to Sign Up:**
- All VAT-registered businesses automatically enrolled
- Must comply from 1st VAT return after 1 April 2022 (deferred from 2019)

### 5.2 MTD Requirements

**Must:**
1. **Keep digital records** - Use MTD-compatible software
2. **Submit VAT returns digitally** - Via software linked to HMRC
3. **Maintain digital links** - No manual data transfer between systems

**MTD-Compatible Software:**
- Xero ✓
- QuickBooks ✓
- Sage ✓
- FreeAgent ✓
- Many others - see HMRC list

**Digital Links:**
- Data must flow digitally between software products
- No manual copying/typing of data
- Spreadsheets can be MTD-compliant if using bridging software

### 5.3 Penalties for Non-Compliance

**From April 2023 - Points-Based Penalty System:**
- Late submission = penalty points
- Reach threshold (varies by filing frequency) = £200 penalty
- Further late returns = additional £200 penalties
- Points expire after period of compliance

**Late Payment Penalties:**
- 15 days late: 2% of VAT owed
- 30 days late: Further 2%
- 31+ days late: 4% per annum on outstanding amount

## 6. CORPORATION TAX

### 6.1 Corporation Tax Basics

**What is Corporation Tax?**
- Tax on company profits
- Includes trading profits, investment income, and capital gains

**Accounting Period:**
- Usually same as financial year (typically 12 months)
- Maximum 12 months (longer periods split)
- Deadline to notify HMRC: 3 months after start of accounting period

### 6.2 Corporation Tax Rates (2025/26)

**Main Rate: 25%**
- Applies to profits over £250,000

**Small Profits Rate: 19%**
- Applies to profits £50,000 or below

**Marginal Relief:**
- Profits between £50,000 and £250,000
- Sliding scale reducing effective rate
- Complex calculation - use HMRC calculator

**Thresholds and Associated Companies:**
- Thresholds divided by (1 + number of associated companies)
- Two associated companies = thresholds halved
- **Associated company:** 50%+ common ownership/control

### 6.3 Corporation Tax Deadlines

| Action | Deadline |
|--------|----------|
| **Notify HMRC** of new accounting period | 3 months after period starts |
| **Pay Corporation Tax** | 9 months + 1 day after accounting period ends |
| **File Company Tax Return (CT600)** | 12 months after accounting period ends |

**Example:**
- Financial year end: 31 December 2025
- Pay tax by: 1 October 2026
- File CT600 by: 31 December 2026

**Large Company Installments:**
- If profits > £1.5m, pay quarterly installments
- Not typical for small/medium companies

### 6.4 Company Tax Return (CT600)

**Must Include:**
- Completed CT600 form
- Statutory accounts
- Corporation Tax computation
- Supporting schedules (if applicable)
- iXBRL-tagged accounts (digital filing format)

**Filing Method:**
- **Online only** via HMRC portal or software
- Must file digitally using iXBRL format
- Cannot file on paper

### 6.5 Allowable Expenses

**Can Deduct:**
- Staff salaries and wages
- Employee pension contributions
- Business travel and subsistence
- Office costs (rent, rates, utilities)
- Professional fees (accountant, legal)
- Marketing and advertising
- Vehicle expenses (business use)
- Depreciation (via capital allowances)
- Bank charges and interest
- Insurance
- Training (job-related)

**Cannot Deduct:**
- Client entertainment
- Dividends paid
- Personal expenses
- Non-trade-related costs
- Depreciation (must use capital allowances instead)

### 6.6 Capital Allowances

**Annual Investment Allowance (AIA):**
- **£1 million** per year (2025/26)
- 100% deduction on qualifying plant & machinery
- Most equipment and business vehicles qualify

**Writing Down Allowances:**
- Main pool: 18% per annum (reducing balance)
- Special rate pool: 6% per annum (integral fixtures, long-life assets)

**Full Expensing (from April 2023):**
- 100% first-year relief on qualifying plant/machinery
- Main rate companies only
- Encourages capital investment

## 7. COMPANIES HOUSE OBLIGATIONS

### 7.1 Annual Accounts Filing

**Requirements:**
- File accounts within **9 months** of financial year end
- First accounts: 21 months after incorporation
- Can file abbreviated accounts (small companies)
- Must file full accounts with shareholders

**Accounts Must Include:**
- Balance Sheet
- Profit & Loss Account (can be abbreviated)
- Notes to accounts
- Directors' Report (unless small company exempt)

**iXBRL Tagging:**
- All accounts must be filed in iXBRL format
- Most accounting software produces this automatically

### 7.2 Confirmation Statement

**What is it?**
- Annual confirmation of company details
- Replaced Annual Return in 2016

**Filing Frequency:**
- At least once every 12 months
- More frequently if details change

**Deadline:**
- 14 days after "confirmation date"
- Confirmation date = anniversary of incorporation or last filing

**Information Confirmed:**
- Registered office address
- Directors and secretaries
- Shareholders
- Share capital
- People with Significant Control (PSC)
- Standard Industrial Classification (SIC) code

**Filing Fee:**
- £34 online
- £62 by post

### 7.3 Changes to Report to Companies House

**Must Report Within 14 Days:**
- Change of directors
- Change of company secretary
- Change of director details (name, address, etc.)
- Change in shareholdings
- Change in people with significant control (PSC)

**Within 15 Days:**
- Changes to articles of association

**Within 21 Days:**
- Change of registered office address
- Appointment/cessation of charges (loans secured on assets)

**Within 28 Days:**
- Issue of new shares

### 7.4 People with Significant Control (PSC) Register

**Who Must Be Registered:**
- Anyone holding more than 25% of shares
- Anyone holding more than 25% of voting rights
- Anyone with right to appoint/remove majority of directors
- Anyone with "significant influence or control"

**Information Required:**
- Full name
- Date of birth
- Nationality
- Correspondence address
- Residential address (not publicly visible)
- Date became PSC
- Nature of control

**PSC Register:**
- Must be kept at registered office or Companies House
- Must be available for public inspection (except residential addresses)

## 8. RECORD KEEPING REQUIREMENTS

### 8.1 Statutory Requirement

**Legal Obligation:**
- Companies Act 2006, Section 386
- VAT Act 1994
- Corporation Tax Act 2009

**Minimum Retention: 6 years**
- From end of financial year they relate to
- Longer if covering multi-year transactions
- Longer if capital assets with useful life > 6 years

### 8.2 Records to Keep

**Financial Records:**
- All sales invoices (copies)
- All purchase invoices and receipts
- Bank statements (all business accounts)
- Credit card statements
- Cash records and petty cash book
- Payroll records (if employees)
- Credit notes issued and received
- VAT records (see section 4.6)
- Stock records
- Asset register

**Company Administration:**
- Certificate of Incorporation
- Articles of Association
- Shareholders' agreements
- Board minutes
- Shareholder resolutions
- Share certificates and register
- PSC register
- All Companies House filings

**Tax Records:**
- All CT600 returns and computations
- All VAT returns
- PAYE records (if employees)
- Capital allowances computations
- Tax correspondence with HMRC

### 8.3 Format of Records

**Acceptable Formats:**
- Paper (original documents)
- Digital scans/photos (if legible and complete)
- Born-digital records (e.g., PDF invoices)
- Accounting software data

**Best Practice:**
- Store digitally with backups
- Cloud-based systems (Xero, etc.) ideal
- Keep physical originals for major documents
- Use consistent filing system

### 8.4 Lost or Destroyed Records

**If Records Lost:**
1. Recreate as best as possible
2. Notify HMRC immediately (in writing)
3. Document circumstances of loss
4. Contact banks for duplicate statements
5. Contact suppliers for invoice copies

**HMRC Powers:**
- Can estimate tax owed if no records
- Penalties for inadequate records
- May increase scrutiny of business

## 9. DIRECTOR RESPONSIBILITIES

### 9.1 Personal Liability

**Directors are personally liable if:**
- Company trades while insolvent
- Fraudulent trading occurs
- Fail to maintain proper records
- Fail to file accounts/returns on time
- Breach of fiduciary duties

**Protection:**
- Directors' and Officers' (D&O) insurance
- Act in good faith at all times
- Seek professional advice when uncertain
- Document decision-making process

### 9.2 Financial Responsibilities

**Must Ensure:**
1. Proper accounting records kept
2. Accounts prepared and filed on time
3. VAT returns filed and paid on time
4. Corporation Tax paid on time
5. All statutory registers maintained
6. Changes notified to Companies House

**Cannot Delegate:**
- Ultimate responsibility remains with directors
- Even if accountant/bookkeeper employed
- "I didn't know" is not a defense

### 9.3 Disqualification

**Grounds for Disqualification:**
- Persistent breach of company law (e.g., late filing)
- Fraud or dishonesty
- Unfit to be director
- Wrongful trading

**Period of Disqualification:**
- Typically 2-15 years
- Cannot act as director or be involved in management
- Serious consequences for business and reputation

### 9.4 Directors' Loans

**Key Rules:**
- Company can lend money to director
- If not repaid within 9 months of year end, triggers tax charge
- Section 455 tax: 33.75% of outstanding balance
- Must be disclosed in accounts
- Subject to benefit-in-kind tax if interest-free or below market rate

**Best Practice:**
- Avoid directors' loans if possible
- If necessary, document properly
- Repay within 9 months
- Charge market rate of interest

## 10. PENALTIES & COMPLIANCE

### 10.1 Late Filing Penalties

**Companies House (Accounts):**
- Up to 1 month late: £150
- 1-3 months late: £375
- 3-6 months late: £750
- Over 6 months late: £1,500

**Repeat Offenders:**
- Penalties doubled for serial late filers
- Possible prosecution
- Director disqualification

**HMRC (Tax Returns):**
- CT600 late: Initial £100, then daily £10 (max £900), then 10-20% of tax
- VAT returns: Points-based system + percentage penalties on tax

### 10.2 Late Payment Penalties

**Corporation Tax:**
- Payment due: 9 months + 1 day after year end
- Interest charged from due date (currently ~7% per annum)
- No automatic penalty but interest adds up

**VAT:**
- New penalty regime from 2023
- 15 days late: 2% of VAT
- 30 days late: Additional 2%
- 31+ days: 4% per annum on outstanding

### 10.3 Errors and Corrections

**Voluntary Disclosure:**
- Correct errors on next return (if under £10,000 net)
- Larger errors: Use VAT652 form or amend CT600
- Penalties reduced for voluntary disclosure

**HMRC Investigations:**
- Can go back 4 years (6 years if careless, 20 if deliberate)
- Penalties up to 100% of tax (200% for offshore)
- Cooperation reduces penalties significantly

**Innocent Errors:**
- No penalty if reasonable care taken
- Must correct promptly once discovered

### 10.4 Record-Keeping Penalties

**Inadequate Records:**
- Up to £3,000 per tax period
- Increased scrutiny from HMRC
- Estimated assessments if cannot prove actual figures
